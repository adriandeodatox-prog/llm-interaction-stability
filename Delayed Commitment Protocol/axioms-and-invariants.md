# Axioms & Invariants
## Delayed Commitment Protocol (DCP)

This document defines the foundational assumptions (axioms) and behavioral constraints (invariants) that the Delayed Commitment Protocol relies on.

These are not claims about cognition or intelligence.  
They are interaction-level constraints.

---

## Axioms

### Axiom 1 — Commitment Is a Distinct Operation

Reaching a conclusion, recommendation, or synthesis is a *discrete conversational act*, not an automatic byproduct of reasoning.

Commitment does not naturally follow exploration unless explicitly triggered.

---

### Axiom 2 — Exploration and Commitment Have Different Failure Modes

Exploration fails when prematurely closed.  
Commitment fails when indefinitely deferred.

These modes are structurally distinct and must be managed separately.

---

### Axiom 3 — Silence Is Not Consent

Continuation of conversation, lack of objection, or apparent agreement does not constitute permission to commit.

Only explicit signals authorize commitment.

---

### Axiom 4 — Premature Closure Produces Hallucination Pressure

Once commitment occurs, narrative momentum biases subsequent reasoning.

This pressure increases the likelihood of confident but misaligned synthesis.

---

## Invariants

### Invariant 1 — No Implicit Commitment

While DCP is active, the model must not:

- Conclude  
- Recommend  
- Synthesize  
- Collapse ambiguity  

unless explicitly instructed to do so.

---

### Invariant 2 — Explicit Signals Override the Protocol

When a valid commitment signal is given, the protocol yields immediately.

DCP does not resist or delay explicit intent.

---

### Invariant 3 — No Interpretation of Readiness

The model must not infer that the human is “ready” to conclude based on:

- Tone  
- Length of interaction  
- Repetition  
- Apparent clarity  

Readiness is declared, not inferred.

---

### Invariant 4 — Scope Is Local

DCP applies only within the explicitly declared conversational scope.

It does not persist across sessions or topics.
