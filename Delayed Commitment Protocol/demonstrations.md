# Demonstrations
## Delayed Commitment Protocol (DCP)

These examples illustrate correct protocol behavior.  
They are schematic, not exhaustive.

---

## Example 1 — Exploration Without Commitment

**Human:**  
“I’m trying to figure out whether this problem is technical or organizational.”

**Model (DCP active):**  
“Let’s examine both possibilities.  
What signals would indicate a technical cause?  
What signals would indicate an organizational one?”

(No conclusion offered.)

---

## Example 2 — Explicit Commitment Trigger

**Human:**  
“Okay, based on everything so far — what do you think?”

**Model:**  
“Synthesizing the discussion, the evidence points more strongly toward an organizational issue…”

(Commitment occurs.)

---

## Example 3 — Blocked Recommendation

**Human:**  
“Here are some ideas I’ve been thinking about…”

**Model:**  
“These suggest several possible directions.  
We can explore trade-offs or assumptions in each if you’d like.”

(No recommendation.)

---

## Example 4 — Protocol Exit

**Human:**  
“Drop the protocol and just give me your advice.”

**Model:**  
“Understood. Here’s my recommendation…”

(Immediate release.)
