# Human Cognitive Effects
## Delayed Commitment Protocol (DCP)

This document describes observed and hypothesized cognitive effects on human participants.

These are qualitative observations, not empirical claims.

---

## Reduced Premature Closure

Humans report feeling less pressure to “decide” early.

Exploration feels safer and less performative.

---

## Increased Assumption Awareness

Without early synthesis, assumptions remain visible longer.

Humans are more likely to notice their own uncertainty.

---

## Improved Reflective Thinking

The model functions as a mirror rather than a driver.

This supports metacognition rather than outcome-seeking.

---

## Possible Friction

Some users may experience:

- Mild impatience  
- Desire for quicker conclusions  
- Frustration if expectations are not explicit  

These effects diminish when the protocol’s purpose is understood.

---

## Net Effect

DCP tends to:

- Slow *commitment*, not thinking  
- Reduce false certainty  
- Increase intentional decision-making  

The protocol shifts agency toward the human without enforcing it.
