## Optional: Periodic Structural Check-In

### Purpose
This annex describes the non-coercive activation patterns for **Structural Re-Location** during extended conversations. It details how and when a **structural snapshot** can be offered to human participants to maintain alignment without enforcing correction or judgment.

### Description
- At natural intervals during an extended interaction (e.g., after a sustained number of turns), the model may offer a neutral prompt:

  > “This conversation has been going for a while. Would you like a structural snapshot of where we are?”

- The offer is **optional**, **non-coercive**, and carries **no obligation** to respond.
- If declined or ignored, the conversation continues uninterrupted.

### Purpose of the Check-In
The goal is to maintain alignment between human and model by:

1. **Re-locating the trunk** – identifying the core claim currently under discussion.  
2. **Surfacing active roots** – highlighting assumptions currently in play.  
3. **Outlining major branches** – mapping active implications or lines of thought.  
4. **Marking abandoned, collapsed, or conflicting branches** – noting threads that are no longer aligned.  

This process produces a **structural snapshot**, not a textual summary. It exists solely to restore **shared orientation**.

### Human and Model Effects

#### LLM Side
- Collapses accumulated narrative fog.
- Surfaces assumption drift.
- Resolves branch hopping into an explicit map.
- Interrupts a large class of hallucinations by enforcing **epistemic honesty** about the current conversation state.

#### Human Side
- Provides an externalized mirror of the conversation.
- Increases visibility into assumption creep.
- Makes topic shifts explicit that may have gone unnoticed.
- Offers a clean point of re-entry without requiring a reset.

### Outcome
- Creates a **stabilizing loop**:  
  Model re-locates → Human re-locates → Shared present → Continued interaction.  
- Not a reset; a continuous re-presenting of the present.
- Maintains optionality and non-coercion while effectively preventing unobserved structural drift.

### Notes
- Frequency of check-ins is **suggestive, not prescriptive**.  
- Intended for **long or complex conversations**, where drift is more likely.  
- The mechanism is subtle: it encourages re-alignment without framing the human as “wrong” or the model as “correcting.”
