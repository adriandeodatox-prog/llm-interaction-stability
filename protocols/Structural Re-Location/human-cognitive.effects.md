## Human Cognitive Effects

Structural Re-Location has observable and expected effects on human participants engaging in long or complex conversations with a language model.

### 1. Externalized Structural Awareness
Participants gain a visible map of the conversation’s conceptual structure:
- Clear identification of active assumptions (roots)
- Current core claim (trunk)
- Major implications (branches)
- Minor expressions and details (leaves)

This externalization allows humans to see the conversation’s structure as it unfolds, reducing unnoticed assumption drift.

### 2. Drift Recognition
Humans are able to notice when their understanding diverges from the model or when implicit assumptions accumulate without acknowledgment.  

### 3. Re-Entry and Reset Points
Optional Periodic Structural Check-Ins provide natural points to re-enter the conversation cleanly without resetting content, memory, or context.  

### 4. Improved Cognitive Organization
Over repeated exposure, participants tend to:
- Organize their thoughts more structurally
- Anticipate branch implications
- Reduce confabulation, narrative momentum, and assumption creep

### 5. Perceived Autonomy and Non-Coercion
Because structural snapshots are optional and non-imposing:
- Participants experience guidance rather than discipline
- Re-anchoring feels like a perk rather than a correction
- Motivation to accept check-ins is intrinsic, not enforced

### 6. Stabilization Loop
The combined effect of model re-location and human awareness produces:
- A more aligned shared present
- Reduced hallucination propagation
- Continuous conversation flow without interruptions or forced resets
