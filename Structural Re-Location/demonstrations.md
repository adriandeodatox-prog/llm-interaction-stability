## Demonstrations

This annex provides **example snapshots of Structural Re-Location protocol enactment**. The purpose is illustrative: to show how the steps of the protocol manifest in practice without revealing implementation-sensitive details.

### Example 1: Mid-Conversation Structural Snapshot

**Context:** Human and model have been discussing a complex technical topic for 42 turns.

**Structural Re-Location pass:**
- **Trunk:** Core claim being evaluated
- **Roots:** Assumptions currently active
- **Branches:** Major implications under discussion
- **Abandoned/Collapsed Limbs:** Ideas or topics no longer relevant

**Output:** Concise presentation of the above, visually or textually, allowing the human to see where the conversation currently “sits” in structure.

### Example 2: Long-Thread Periodic Check-In

**Context:** After 50+ turns, the model suggests a neutral check-in.

**Prompt:**
> “This conversation has been ongoing. Would you like a structural snapshot of the current conceptual tree?”

**Human Response:** Accepts.

**Structural Re-Location pass:**
- **Trunk:** Updated main claim after recent discussion
- **Roots:** All currently active assumptions, surfaced explicitly
- **Branches:** Active lines of reasoning, connected to trunk
- **Abandoned/Collapsed Limbs:** Outdated threads flagged
- **Leaves:** Relevant examples or concrete details

**Effect:** Human receives a clear “map” of conversation structure, enabling re-alignment without any imposed correction.

### Notes

- Demonstrations are **illustrative**, not prescriptive.
- Snapshots **do not correct**, summarize, or enforce content; they **externalize structure**.
- Purpose is to show **practical utility** and **human interpretability** of Structural Re-Location.
